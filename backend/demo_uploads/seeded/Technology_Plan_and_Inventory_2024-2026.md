## 1. Technology Vision

Lincoln Innovation Academy believes technology should be a tool that amplifies great teaching and deepens student learning, not a replacement for human connection. Our technology program supports the school's mission of innovation by providing students with the digital literacy skills and computational thinking abilities they need for success in the 21st century, while maintaining our commitment to balanced, developmentally appropriate screen time.

### Guiding Principles
1. Technology serves pedagogy, not the other way around
2. Equitable access for all students regardless of home resources
3. Digital citizenship is taught explicitly and reinforced across the curriculum
4. Age-appropriate use with increasing independence as students mature
5. Data privacy and student safety are non-negotiable

| Grade Band | Topics | Curriculum Resource | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|

| 3-5 | Digital footprint, cyberbullying prevention, evaluating online sources, screen time balance, password safety | Common Sense Media 3-5 units | Monthly lessons (classroom teacher) |
| 6-8 | Social media responsibility, news literacy, online privacy and data, digital drama and cyberbullying, AI literacy basics | Common Sense Media 6-8 units + teacher-developed AI unit | Bi-monthly lessons + integration in ELA |

## 6. Data Privacy and Security

- All educational technology vendors must sign a Student Data Privacy Agreement (SDPA) compliant with Minnesota Student Data Privacy Act
- Student data is never sold or used for advertising
- Vendors are reviewed annually through the Student Data Privacy Consortium (SDPC)
- Parent notification and opt-out procedures are communicated at Back-to-School Night and available on school website
- Staff complete annual data privacy training (October each year)
- Incident response plan on file with technology specialist and principal

## 7. Key Priorities for 2024-2026

1. **Formalize device refresh cycle:** Establish 4-year replacement cycle for all student devices with dedicated annual budget line
2. **Expand STEM programming:** Add dedicated STEM/CS class time in middle school schedule (currently only through integration)
3. **AI literacy:** Develop grade-appropriate AI literacy units for grades 5-8 in response to growing student use of AI tools
4. **Home connectivity:** Partner with Comcast Internet Essentials and PCs for People to close digital divide for 20+ families without reliable home internet
5. **Assessment of 1:1 model for grades 3-4:** Evaluate whether transitioning from cart model to assigned devices improves learning outcomes
6. **Assistive technology expansion:** Work with SpEd coordinator to identify and procure additional assistive technology tools
