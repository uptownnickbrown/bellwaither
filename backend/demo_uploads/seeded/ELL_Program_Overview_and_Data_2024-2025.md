## Program Overview

Lincoln Innovation Academy serves 97 English Language Learners, representing 23.1% of our total enrollment of 420 students. Our ELL program uses a combination of push-in/co-teaching support, pull-out small group instruction, and sheltered content instruction to ensure all multilingual students access grade-level content while developing English proficiency.

### Staffing Concern

The current ELL teacher-to-student ratio (1:97) exceeds recommended levels. Minnesota does not mandate specific ratios, but professional organizations recommend 1:35-50 for direct service. The addition of Ms. Hassan as a paraprofessional has helped, but a second certified ELL teacher is needed, particularly as the ELL population has grown from 18% to 23% of enrollment in three years.

**Recommendation:** Hire a 0.5 FTE ELL teacher for 2025-26, focusing on grades 4-8 where content demands are highest and students have the most complex language needs. Estimated cost: $30,000-$35,000.

## Service Delivery Model

### K-2 (36 ELL students)
- **Levels 1-2 (8 students):** Pull-out small group (30 min/day) for targeted English language development + push-in support during literacy block (45 min/day)
- **Levels 3-4 (18 students):** Push-in support during literacy block (30 min/day) + weekly pull-out vocabulary/language group (30 min, 2x/week)
- **Level 5 (10 students):** Monitoring with periodic push-in support; classroom teacher implements SIOP strategies

### 3-5 (33 ELL students)
- **Levels 1-2 (9 students):** Pull-out small group (30 min/day) for ELD + push-in during ELA and content classes (varies)
- **Levels 3-4 (17 students):** Push-in support during ELA (30 min/day) + content vocabulary pre-teaching (2x/week)
- **Level 5 (7 students):** Monitoring; consultation with classroom teachers on accommodation needs

### 6-8 (28 ELL students)
- **Levels 1-2 (9 students):** Pull-out ELD class (45 min/day, replaces one elective) + push-in during core content classes
- **Levels 3-4 (15 students):** Push-in support during ELA (30 min/day) + sheltered content strategies implemented by classroom teachers
- **Level 5 (4 students):** Monitoring status; annual WIDA ACCESS assessment

### Reclassification Data

| Year | Students Reclassified (Exited ELL) | Total ELL at Start of Year | Reclassification Rate |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2021-22 | 4 | 56 | 7.1% |

Reclassification rate is improving steadily. Students reclassified in the past 2 years have maintained academic performance in general education, with 85% meeting grade-level expectations on MAP one year post-reclassification.

### MAP Achievement (ELL vs. All Students, Winter 2025)

| Assessment | ELL Mean RIT | All Students Mean RIT | Gap |
|---|---|---|---|
| Reading K-2 | 155 | 168 | 13 |
| Reading 3-5 | 192 | 205 | 13 |
| Reading 6-8 | 206 | 220 | 14 |
| Math K-2 | 162 | 170 | 8 |
| Math 3-5 | 200 | 210 | 10 |
| Math 6-8 | 215 | 226 | 11 |

**Observations:**
- Reading gaps are larger than math gaps, as expected for language learners
- Math gaps widening in upper grades suggests increasing language demands in math content
- Growth rates for ELL students are strong (64% meeting MAP reading growth targets; 56% meeting math growth targets), outperforming state ELL averages

## Family Engagement for ELL Families

- Back-to-School Night: multilingual welcome and program overview sessions held in Spanish and Somali
- Parent-teacher conferences: interpreters provided for all ELL families (see conference attendance data)
- ELL Family Night: held November 2024 (42 families attended); topics included supporting literacy at home, understanding WIDA levels, and navigating school systems
- Monthly multilingual parent communication: key school updates translated and sent in Spanish, Somali, Hmong, and Karen
- 7 ELL families connected to NAZ family coaching services
- 4 ELL families received technology support through Minnesota Computers for Schools partnership

## Priorities for 2025-2026

1. Hire additional ELL teacher (0.5 FTE) to reduce caseload ratio
2. Provide SIOP (Sheltered Instruction Observation Protocol) training for all classroom teachers
3. Expand home language literacy support (partner with community organizations for native language reading groups)
4. Improve writing proficiency among ELL students (lowest ACCESS domain)
5. Develop newcomer protocol for students arriving with interrupted formal education (SIFE)
6. Increase ELL family survey response rates through phone-based and in-person survey options
