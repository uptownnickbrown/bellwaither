## Updated: January 2025

## Overview

Lincoln Innovation Academy maintains formal and informal partnerships with 14 community organizations to support our mission of providing innovative, whole-child education. This document summarizes active partnership agreements, their scope, and impact data.

**Partnership Coordinator:** Tamara Williams, Family & Community Liaison
**Administrative Oversight:** Dr. Angela Rivera, Principal

## Tier 1 Partnerships (Formal MOUs, Significant Scope)

### 1. University of Minnesota - College of Education and Human Development

**MOU Effective:** August 2022 - July 2025 (renewal in progress)
**Primary Contact:** Dr. Patricia Nguyen, Clinical Faculty Director
**Partnership Scope:**
- Student teacher placement site (4-6 student teachers per year)
- Action research collaboration with graduate students
- Professional development guest facilitation (2-3 sessions per year)
- Pipeline for teacher recruitment

**2024-25 Activity:**
- 5 student teachers placed (2 in K-2, 1 in grade 4, 1 in grade 6 math, 1 in grade 7 ELA)
- 2 graduate research projects conducted (literacy intervention effectiveness; restorative practices implementation)
- Dr. Nguyen facilitated November PD session on formative assessment

**Impact:** 3 of our current teachers (8.5% of teaching staff) were originally student teachers at LIA. This partnership is our strongest recruitment pipeline.

**Cost to LIA:** None (in-kind: mentor teacher time)
**Value Received:** Estimated $45,000 in student teacher instructional hours; $6,000 in PD facilitation

### 2. Northside Achievement Zone (NAZ)

**MOU Effective:** July 2021 - June 2025
**Primary Contact:** Marcus Thompson, School Partnership Manager
**Partnership Scope:**
- Family coaching and support services for eligible families
- College-readiness programming for grades 6-8
- Scholarship navigation support for 8th grade families
- Data sharing for enrolled families (with consent)

**2024-25 Activity:**
- 32 LIA families currently enrolled in NAZ coaching (up from 24 last year)
- 18 middle school students participating in NAZ college readiness program
- 2 family coaching workshops held on-site at LIA (financial literacy, navigating high school choice)
- 6 families received support with high school application process

**Impact:** Families enrolled in NAZ coaching report higher satisfaction with school communication (4.5 vs 4.1 average) and attend more school events (avg 5.2 vs 3.8 events/year).

**Cost to LIA:** None
**Value Received:** Estimated $48,000 in family support services

---

### 3. Second Harvest Heartland

**MOU Effective:** Ongoing since 2020, renewed annually
**Primary Contact:** Keisha Davis, School Programs Coordinator
**Partnership Scope:**
- Weekend food backpack program for identified students
- Holiday food distribution events (Thanksgiving, winter break, spring break)
- Summer meal site coordination

**2024-25 Activity:**
- 65 students receiving weekly food backpacks (15.5% of enrollment)
- Thanksgiving distribution served 48 families
- Winter break distribution served 52 families
- Summer meal site operated June-August 2024 serving average 85 meals/day

**Impact:** Teachers report fewer Monday hunger-related behavior and focus issues among participating students. Nurse referrals for hunger-related complaints decreased 40% since program inception.

**Cost to LIA:** $1,200/year (storage, distribution coordination staff time)
**Value Received:** Estimated $52,000 in food assistance

---

### 4. Upstream Arts

**MOU Effective:** September 2023 - June 2025
**Primary Contact:** Jamie Lindstrom, Education Programs Director
**Partnership Scope:**
- 8-week arts residency for grades 3-5 (fall) and grades 6-8 (spring)
- Integration of arts with core academic content
- Student showcase performance
- Teacher professional development on arts integration

**2024-25 Activity:**
- Fall residency completed: 3rd-5th grade students created community mural exploring identity and belonging
- Spring residency scheduled: 6th-8th grade theater arts program on social justice themes
- 2 teachers participated in arts integration PD workshop

**Impact:** Students in residency grades showed 12% increase in school belonging measures on Panorama survey. Art teacher reports increased student engagement during and after residency periods.

**Cost to LIA:** $8,500/year (subsidized rate; full cost would be $15,000)
**Value Received:** $15,000 in programming

### 5. YMCA of the Greater Twin Cities

**MOU Effective:** August 2023 - July 2026
**Primary Contact:** DeShawn Carter, Youth Development Director
**Partnership Scope:**
- After-school programming Monday-Thursday, 3:30-5:30 PM
- Summer day camp (4 weeks in June-July)
- Gym and pool access for LIA students and families
- Staff wellness program (discounted memberships)

**2024-25 Activity:**
- Average daily after-school attendance: 62 students (14.8% of enrollment)
- Programming includes homework help, recreation, STEM clubs, and arts
- Summer camp served 45 students in 2024
- 12 staff members utilizing wellness membership discount

**Impact:** Students in after-school program have 15% fewer unexcused absences than school average. Parent survey: 94% satisfaction with program quality.

**Cost to LIA:** $22,000/year (facility use fee, coordination)
**Value Received:** Estimated $120,000 in after-school and summer programming
