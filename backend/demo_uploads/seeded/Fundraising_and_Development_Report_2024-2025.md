## Executive Summary

Lincoln Innovation Academy has raised $142,800 year-to-date against a full-year goal of $195,000, representing 73.2% of target with 58% of the fiscal year elapsed. We are on pace to meet or slightly exceed our annual goal. Key drivers include a strong Annual Fund campaign, a successful fall gala, and a new corporate matching program. Areas of focus for the remainder of the year include grant applications and the spring Give to the Max Day campaign.

## Key Challenges and Recommendations

1. **Grant capacity:** We lack a dedicated development staff person. All grant writing is done by board members and the principal, limiting our capacity. Recommendation: Budget for a part-time development coordinator in FY26 ($25,000-$30,000).

2. **Donor diversification:** Current parent giving is the largest source, which creates risk as families turn over. Need to grow alumni, community, and corporate base. Recommendation: Launch alumni giving program as first cohorts reach college age.

3. **Restricted vs. unrestricted:** 42% of funds raised are restricted to specific purposes. While this is normal, it limits flexibility. Recommendation: Emphasize unrestricted giving in spring appeal messaging.

4. **Database management:** Donor records are maintained in spreadsheets, which is error-prone. Recommendation: Invest in donor management software (e.g., Little Green Light, ~$600/year) to improve tracking and stewardship.
