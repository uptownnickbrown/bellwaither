3. PBL UNIT PLANNING - Q3
====================================================================

The team discussed the Q3 Project-Based Learning unit. Per the school-wide PBL framework, each grade level designs one major project per quarter integrating multiple subject areas.

Q3 PBL Theme: "Innovators in Our Community"

Overview: Students will research a local community challenge, interview community members, design a solution prototype, and present their work at the spring PBL Showcase (May 15).

Timeline:
- Weeks 1-2 (Jan 21 - Jan 31): Introduction, community challenge identification, research skills
- Weeks 3-4 (Feb 3 - Feb 14): Community interviews and primary source research
- Weeks 5-6 (Feb 18 - Feb 28): Solution design and prototyping
- Weeks 7-8 (Mar 3 - Mar 14): Revision and presentation preparation
- Showcase: May 15 (final presentations to community audience)

Standards addressed:
- ELA: RI.5.7 (multiple sources), W.5.7 (research), SL.5.4 (presentation)
- Math: 5.MD.1 (measurement), 5.NF (fractions in real-world contexts - if applicable)
- Science: 5-ESS3-1 (human impact on environment)
- Social Studies: Community and civic engagement standards

Tamika has a contact at the Ramsey County Sustainability Office who is willing to host a virtual Q&A session with students. Roberto knows a community organizer at the Frogtown neighborhood association who could be a guest speaker.

Discussion about how to differentiate the project for students with IEPs. Both teachers will consult with Jennifer Walsh (SpEd teacher) about appropriate scaffolds. Sarah suggested using graphic organizers for the research phase and allowing multiple presentation formats (oral, poster, video).

The team also discussed concerns about equitable access to community interview opportunities. Some students may not have family connections to community leaders. Tamika suggested creating a "Community Expert Panel" of 5-6 volunteers who would be available for all students to interview, ensuring no one is left out.
