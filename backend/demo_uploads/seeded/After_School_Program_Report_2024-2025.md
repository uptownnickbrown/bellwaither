## Program Overview

Lincoln Innovation Academy's after-school program, operated in partnership with the YMCA of the Greater Twin Cities, provides academic support, enrichment, and recreation for students in grades K-8. Now in its second year, the program serves as a critical support for working families and provides extended learning opportunities aligned with the school's mission.

## Program Components

### 1. Homework Help and Academic Support (3:30 - 4:15 PM daily)

All students begin with a structured homework help period. YMCA staff and LIA teachers (2 teachers stay for after-school support on rotating basis through stipend program) circulate to assist students.

**Key metrics:**
- Average homework completion rate during program: 78%
- Students receiving after-school program support showed 12% fewer missing assignments than peers (per teacher reports)
- 8 students receive targeted reading intervention support from LIA reading specialist (funded separately through Title I)

## Family Feedback

From mid-year survey (January 2025, 52 responses out of 95 families = 54.7% response rate):

| Question | Strongly Agree | Agree | Neutral | Disagree | Strongly Disagree |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| My child enjoys the after-school program | 42% | 44% | 10% | 4% | 0% |
| The program helps my child complete homework | 38% | 46% | 12% | 4% | 0% |
| The program provides meaningful enrichment | 36% | 40% | 18% | 6% | 0% |
| My child feels safe in the program | 52% | 42% | 4% | 2% | 0% |
| Staff communicate well with families | 30% | 38% | 22% | 8% | 2% |
| I would recommend the program to other families | 40% | 44% | 12% | 4% | 0% |
| The program helps me manage my work schedule | 58% | 34% | 6% | 2% | 0% |

**Overall satisfaction: 4.2 / 5.0**

Open-ended feedback themes:
- Appreciation for affordable, quality programming (most common positive theme)
- Request for Friday programming (currently Mon-Thu only)
- Some parents want more structured academic time, less free play
- Communication about daily activities could be improved (requested daily text/app update)
- Several families mentioned the program is essential for their ability to work

## Academic Impact

Preliminary analysis comparing after-school program participants to non-participants (controlling for baseline achievement):

| Metric | Participants (n=62 avg daily) | Non-Participants | Difference |
|---|---|---|---|
| MAP Reading Fall-to-Winter Growth (RIT) | +5.8 | +4.9 | +0.9 |
| MAP Math Fall-to-Winter Growth (RIT) | +5.2 | +4.6 | +0.6 |
| Missing Assignment Rate | 8.2% | 12.4% | -4.2 pp |
| Unexcused Absence Rate | 2.8% | 4.1% | -1.3 pp |
| Office Discipline Referral Rate | 6.8 per 100 | 9.2 per 100 | -2.4 per 100 |

Note: These are correlational findings and should not be interpreted as causal. Students who attend after-school programming may differ from non-participants in ways not captured by this analysis.
