# Lincoln Innovation Academy
# Facility Condition Assessment Report
## Annual Assessment - October 2024

**Conducted by:** Derek Olson, Operations Manager
**External Assessment:** Northland Building Consultants (structural and HVAC)
**Approved by:** Dr. Angela Rivera, Principal
**Building Address:** 1500 Innovation Drive, St. Paul, MN 55108
**Building Type:** Leased facility (converted commercial/office space, renovated 2019)
**Total Square Footage:** 38,000 sq ft
**Year Built:** 1985 | Renovated for school use: 2019
**Lease Term:** 10-year lease, expires June 2029 (5-year renewal option)

---

## Overall Building Rating: B- (Satisfactory with Notable Concerns)

The building is generally in good condition for its age and use. The 2019 renovation successfully converted the space for K-8 school use. However, the building is showing wear consistent with 5 years of intensive school use, and several systems are approaching the point where significant investment will be needed. The growing enrollment is also straining some spaces not originally designed for this capacity.

### 2. HVAC (Heating, Ventilation, Air Conditioning)

**Rating: C+**

| Component | Condition | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Heating (gas-fired rooftop units) | Fair | 4 rooftop units from original 1985 construction (replaced condenser coils 2019). Running less efficiently. Two units cycling excessively. |
| Cooling | Fair | Same rooftop units. Cooling capacity barely adequate for current occupancy. Wing B (3rd-5th grade) consistently 3-5 degrees warmer than setpoint on warm days. |
| Ventilation/Air Quality | Fair | CO2 levels measured above 1000 ppm in 3 classrooms during peak occupancy (Target: <800 ppm). Portable air purifiers added to those rooms as interim measure. |
| Controls/Thermostat | Fair | Original programmable thermostats. No centralized building management system. Individual room temperature control is limited. |
| Air Filtration | Good | MERV-13 filters installed post-COVID. Changed on 90-day schedule. |

**Recommended Actions:**
- Replace 2 oldest rooftop HVAC units (Priority: High, Est. Cost: $45,000-$55,000 per unit). These are 39 years old and beyond expected lifespan. Budget for replacement within 2 years.
- Install centralized building management system for HVAC (Priority: Medium, Est. Cost: $15,000). Would improve energy efficiency 15-20% and allow remote monitoring.
- Add supplementary ventilation to 3 high-CO2 classrooms (Priority: High, Est. Cost: $8,000). Energy recovery ventilators recommended.
- Consider mini-split supplementary cooling for Wing B (Priority: Medium, Est. Cost: $12,000)

**Note:** HVAC is the single largest facility concern. Replacement of aging units should be coordinated with landlord per lease terms (Section 8.3 - landlord responsible for major mechanical systems; tenant responsible for maintenance).

## Capacity Analysis

| Space | Current Use | Capacity | Current Utilization | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| K-2 Classrooms (6) | 165 students | 150 optimal / 180 max | 110% of optimal | Class sizes slightly above ideal for early elementary |
| 3-5 Classrooms (6) | 155 students | 156 optimal / 180 max | 99% of optimal | At capacity |
| 6-8 Classrooms (6) | 162 students | 168 optimal / 192 max | 96% of optimal | Adequate but no room for growth |
| Cafeteria | 3 lunch periods | 120 per seating | 100% | At capacity with 3 seatings. 4th seating would disrupt schedule. |
| Gymnasium | PE, assemblies, after-school | 200 assembly | 100% (assemblies) | Cannot hold all-school assembly. Must split K-4 and 5-8. |
| Media Center | Library, small groups | 30 | 80% | Adequate but also used for pull-out services, reducing availability |
| STEM Lab/Computer Lab | Technology classes | 24 | 100% | No room for expansion |
| Conference Room (1) | Meetings, IEP meetings | 10 | Overbooked | Frequently double-booked. Need additional meeting space. |
| Staff Lounge | Staff breaks | 15 | Adequate | Small but functional |
| Administrative Offices | 4 offices | 4 people | 100% | Principal, assistant, coaches share space |
| Counseling Offices (2) | Counseling, social work | 3 staff | Over capacity | 3 counseling/SW staff share 2 small offices |

**Key Finding:** The building is at or near functional capacity at current enrollment of 420 students. Any enrollment growth would require either:
1. Additional leased space (adjacent suite may become available - landlord notified of interest)
2. Creative scheduling to maximize use of existing spaces
3. Outdoor learning space development (currently underutilized)
