## Executive Summary

Lincoln Innovation Academy currently serves 58 students with Individualized Education Programs (IEPs), representing 13.8% of our total enrollment of 420 students. An additional 14 students receive services under Section 504 accommodation plans. This report provides a comprehensive review of our special education program's compliance status, staffing, service delivery, and areas for improvement.

Overall, Lincoln Innovation Academy maintains strong compliance with IDEA requirements, with a 96% on-time IEP completion rate and zero state audit findings in the 2023-24 review cycle. However, increasing caseloads and growing student needs require attention to staffing and resource allocation for the coming school year.

### Staffing Concerns

Both special education teachers are currently carrying caseloads that exceed the recommended maximum of 25 students. This has resulted in:
- Reduced time for progress monitoring and data collection
- Difficulty scheduling all required IEP meetings within compliance timelines
- Limited capacity for co-teaching and push-in service delivery
- Teacher stress and burnout concerns (reflected in staff satisfaction survey data)

**Recommendation:** Hire one additional special education teacher for the 2025-26 school year, ideally with K-4 certification, to redistribute caseloads and enable more inclusive service delivery models. Estimated cost: $55,000-$65,000 salary plus benefits.

### Compliance Notes

Two annual IEP reviews were completed 3-5 days late due to scheduling conflicts with families. In both cases, documented attempts to schedule were on file and services continued without interruption. The parent notification metric fell below target primarily during the October-November period when conference scheduling was particularly challenging. We have since implemented automated notification through our SIS system to improve this metric.

### State Monitoring

Lincoln Innovation Academy received no findings in the most recent Minnesota Department of Education compliance monitoring review (Spring 2024). The school was commended for:
- Thorough documentation of prereferral interventions
- High-quality IEP goal writing with measurable objectives
- Strong parent participation rates in IEP meetings (92% attendance)

### Pre-Referral Intervention (MTSS/RTI)

All referrals for special education evaluation were preceded by documented Tier 2 and Tier 3 interventions through our MTSS framework, with the following documentation:
- Universal screening data (FAST/aReading, FAST/aMath administered 3x/year)
- Tier 2 intervention logs with progress monitoring data (minimum 6 weeks)
- Tier 3 intervention logs with progress monitoring data (minimum 4-6 weeks)
- Parent notification of interventions and progress
