## Crisis Communications Protocol

### Principles
1. Communicate early, even if information is incomplete ("We are aware of... and will share more as we know it")
2. Be honest and transparent
3. Prioritize student safety information
4. Use multiple channels to reach all families
5. Principal is sole spokesperson for media
6. Review all external communications before sending (two-person review)

### Crisis Communication Chain
1. Incident occurs; principal notified
2. Principal determines communication level:
   - **Level 1** (routine): Teacher/office handles (e.g., individual student illness)
   - **Level 2** (notable): Principal sends targeted communication (e.g., confirmed lice case, water main break)
   - **Level 3** (significant): Principal sends all-family communication within 1 hour (e.g., security incident, severe weather event, staff departure)
   - **Level 4** (critical): Principal sends immediate all-family communication + contacts Board Chair + prepares media statement (e.g., active threat, serious injury, major legal issue)
3. For Levels 3-4: ParentSquare notification + email + website update
4. Designated bilingual staff translate critical messages within 30 minutes
5. Follow-up communication within 24 hours with additional information and resources

### Social Media Crisis Protocol
- Monitor school mentions daily (Tamara Williams)
- Do not engage with negative posts publicly; respond via private message
- For misinformation: Post factual correction on official channels
- For threats or harassment: Document, report to platform, notify law enforcement if warranted
- No staff should respond to media inquiries via social media - direct to principal

## Equity in Communications

### Language Access Standards
- All school-wide communications available in English, Spanish, and Somali (our three largest language groups, covering 92% of families)
- Hmong and Karen translation available for critical communications and upon request
- ParentSquare auto-translate feature enabled for all other languages
- Interpreter services available for all family meetings (scheduled 48 hours in advance when possible)
- Bilingual staff: Tamara Williams (Spanish), Aisha Mohammed (Somali, intern), Mai Xiong (Hmong, ELL teacher)

### Accessibility Standards
- All digital communications meet WCAG 2.1 AA standards
- Documents shared as accessible PDFs when possible
- Video content captioned
- Printed materials available upon request for families without digital access
- Phone tree for emergency communications to families without smartphones (12 families currently)

### Inclusive Communication Practices
- Use family-friendly language; avoid educational jargon
- Lead with strengths and assets, not deficits
- Represent student and family diversity in photos and stories (with consent)
- Acknowledge and celebrate the cultural diversity of our community
- Ensure communications do not assume two-parent households, English proficiency, or digital access
