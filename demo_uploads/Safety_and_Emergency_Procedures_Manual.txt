1. GENERAL SAFETY PROTOCOLS
====================================================================

Lincoln Innovation Academy is committed to providing a safe and secure learning environment for all students, staff, and visitors. All staff members are responsible for maintaining awareness of safety procedures and participating in all required training and drills.

Key principles:
- Safety is everyone's responsibility
- When in doubt, report concerns to administration immediately
- Follow established protocols; do not improvise unless lives are at immediate risk
- Account for all students at all times during emergency situations
- Maintain calm, clear communication

All staff complete the following safety training annually:
- Emergency procedures overview (August professional development)
- CPR/First Aid certification (required for all classroom teachers; provided biannually)
- Mandatory reporter training (required by Minnesota law)
- Blood-borne pathogen training
- De-escalation and crisis intervention (CPI training for identified staff)

2. EMERGENCY COMMUNICATION
====================================================================

INTERNAL COMMUNICATION:
- Primary: Intercom/PA system (all classrooms and common areas)
- Secondary: Two-way radios (administration, office staff, custodial, specials teachers)
- Tertiary: Cell phones (text-based group message via admin team group)
- Silent alert: Email with subject line "EMERGENCY" sent from front office

Code language for PA announcements:
- "Code Red" = Lockdown (threat inside building)
- "Code Yellow" = Lockout (threat outside building, secure perimeter)
- "Code Green" = Evacuation
- "Code Blue" = Medical emergency (location will be specified)
- "Code Gray" = Severe weather shelter

EXTERNAL COMMUNICATION:
- 911 called immediately for any life-threatening emergency
- ParentSquare: Mass notification to all families (text, email, app notification)
- School website: Updated with information during extended emergencies
- Social media: Official updates posted only by principal or designee
- Media inquiries: Directed to principal only; no staff should speak to media

FAMILY REUNIFICATION NOTIFICATION:
- ParentSquare message with reunification site location and procedures
- Families directed NOT to come to school building during active emergencies
- Reunification site: Grace Lutheran Church, 1247 Elm Street (0.3 miles from school)
- Secondary site: Roosevelt Community Center, 890 Oak Avenue (0.5 miles from school)

3. FIRE EMERGENCY / EVACUATION
====================================================================

SIGNAL: Continuous fire alarm (horn and strobe lights)

PROCEDURES:
1. Teacher: Grab red emergency folder (contains class roster, emergency contacts, first aid supplies)
2. Students line up immediately; no personal belongings
3. Exit building using designated route posted by classroom door
4. Teacher is LAST person out; close door behind you (do not lock)
5. Proceed to designated assembly area on field (minimum 300 feet from building)
6. Teacher: Take attendance using roster; report ANY missing students immediately to grade-level lead
7. Grade-level leads report to principal at command post
8. Remain at assembly area until "ALL CLEAR" announcement

EVACUATION ROUTES:
- K-2 classrooms (Wing A): Exit through Door A1 (east), assemble at east field
- 3-5 classrooms (Wing B): Exit through Door B1 (south), assemble at south parking lot
- 6-8 classrooms (Wing C): Exit through Door C1 (west), assemble at west field
- Office/Common areas: Exit through main entrance (north), assemble at front sidewalk
- Cafeteria: Exit through cafeteria emergency exit (south), assemble at south parking lot
- Gymnasium: Exit through gym emergency exit (east), assemble at east field

STUDENTS WITH MOBILITY NEEDS:
- Evacuation chairs stationed at top of each stairwell
- Designated staff trained on evacuation chair operation:
  * Wing A: Derek Olson (custodial) and Ms. Patel (1st grade)
  * Wing C: Mr. Foster (8th grade) and Marcus Johnson (para)
- Students with mobility needs have individualized evacuation plans on file

4. LOCKDOWN PROCEDURES (Code Red)
====================================================================

SIGNAL: PA announcement "Code Red, Code Red, Code Red" followed by location if known

Used when: Threat is INSIDE the building

PROCEDURES:
1. LOCKS: Lock classroom door immediately
2. LIGHTS: Turn off lights
3. OUT OF SIGHT: Move students away from doors and windows to designated safe corner
4. QUIET: Maintain silence; silence all cell phones
5. Teacher: Take attendance silently; note any missing or extra students
6. Do NOT open door for anyone until "ALL CLEAR" from administration or law enforcement
7. Cover door window if possible
8. If students are in hallway: Enter nearest room immediately
9. If students are in restroom: Lock stall, stand on toilet, remain silent
10. If outside: Do NOT re-enter building; move to off-site evacuation point

ADMINISTRATION:
- Call 911 immediately
- Lock exterior doors remotely (office can engage magnetic locks)
- Send ParentSquare notification to families
- Direct law enforcement upon arrival

SPECIAL CONSIDERATIONS:
- Cafeteria: Kitchen staff lock kitchen doors; students shelter in place in cafeteria with staff
- Gymnasium: Coach locks exterior doors; students move to locker rooms
- Playground: Staff move students to Grace Lutheran Church (reunification site); do NOT re-enter building

5. SEVERE WEATHER / TORNADO (Code Gray)
====================================================================

SIGNAL: PA announcement "Code Gray, Code Gray, Code Gray"

SHELTER LOCATIONS (interior rooms, lowest floor, away from windows):
- K-2: Hallway A (interior corridor), students sit against wall in tornado position
- 3-5: Hallway B (interior corridor), students sit against wall in tornado position
- 6-8: Lower level corridor near gym locker rooms
- Office staff: Interior conference room
- Cafeteria: Students move to lower level corridor

PROCEDURES:
1. Move students quickly and calmly to shelter location
2. Students assume tornado position: sitting, facing wall, hands over head and neck
3. Teacher: Bring red emergency folder and take attendance
4. Close all doors between shelter area and exterior rooms
5. Stay away from windows, skylights, and exterior doors
6. Remain in shelter until "ALL CLEAR" from administration

TORNADO WATCH vs. WARNING:
- Watch: Be prepared. Review shelter locations with students. Monitor weather radio.
- Warning: Immediately execute Code Gray procedures. Do not wait for PA announcement if tornado is visible.

Weather monitoring: NOAA weather radio in main office (always on during severe weather season, March-September). Derek Olson monitors weather alerts and notifies principal.

6. MEDICAL EMERGENCY (Code Blue)
====================================================================

PROCEDURES:
1. Assess scene safety; do not move injured person unless in immediate danger
2. Call front office: "Code Blue in [location]"
3. Office calls 911 if needed and dispatches trained staff to location
4. Certified staff begin first aid/CPR as needed
5. Send other students away from scene with another adult
6. Retrieve AED if cardiac emergency (AED locations: main office, gymnasium, cafeteria)
7. Designate someone to meet ambulance at main entrance and guide to location
8. Notify parent/guardian immediately
9. Document incident on medical incident form within 24 hours

LIFE-THREATENING ALLERGIES:
- 14 students have life-threatening allergies (list maintained by school nurse and in each teacher's emergency folder)
- EpiPens: Student-specific EpiPens stored in nurse's office and classroom teacher's emergency kit
- All staff trained on EpiPen administration during August PD
- Nut-aware school: No nut products in K-2 classrooms; nut-free table in cafeteria

SEIZURE PROTOCOL:
- 3 students with seizure disorders (action plans in emergency folders)
- Time the seizure; call office if >3 minutes or first-time seizure
- Clear area around student; do not restrain; place on side after seizure ends
- Follow individual student seizure action plan

7. HAZARDOUS MATERIALS
====================================================================

If chemical spill or hazardous material release is reported:
1. Evacuate affected area immediately
2. Notify office; office calls 911
3. If INDOOR spill: Evacuate building (Code Green)
4. If OUTDOOR release: Shelter in place (close windows, shut off HVAC if possible)
5. Do not attempt to clean up unknown substances
6. Account for all students
7. Follow direction of fire department HazMat team upon arrival

Chemical storage: All cleaning chemicals stored in locked custodial closet. Science lab chemicals stored in locked cabinet with inventory list. MSDS sheets available in custodial office and science lab.

====================================================================
8. BOMB THREAT
====================================================================

If threat received by phone:
1. Keep caller on line as long as possible
2. Note exact words, background sounds, caller characteristics
3. Signal another staff member to call 911
4. Complete Bomb Threat Checklist (located in main office binder)
5. Do NOT use cell phones or two-way radios (potential detonation trigger)
6. Principal determines evacuation based on law enforcement guidance
7. Evacuation to off-site location (Grace Lutheran Church)

If threat received in writing or electronically:
1. Do not handle the document further; preserve for law enforcement
2. Notify principal immediately
3. Call 911
4. Follow law enforcement guidance

====================================================================
9. ACTIVE THREAT (Run, Hide, Fight)
====================================================================

In an active threat situation where lockdown is not possible or has been breached:

RUN: If safe to do so, evacuate the building immediately
- Leave belongings behind
- Help others if possible without endangering yourself
- Do not attempt to move wounded people
- Keep hands visible when encountering law enforcement
- Proceed to off-site reunification point

HIDE: If evacuation is not possible
- Find a room that locks; barricade the door
- Hide behind large items
- Silence phones
- Remain quiet and out of sight

FIGHT: As an absolute last resort
- Act with aggression; commit to your actions
- Use improvised weapons (fire extinguisher, chairs, books)
- Work as a team if possible

Staff who have completed ALICE or similar training: 8 (target: all staff by end of 2025-26)

10. REUNIFICATION PROCEDURES
====================================================================

PRIMARY REUNIFICATION SITE: Grace Lutheran Church, 1247 Elm Street
SECONDARY SITE: Roosevelt Community Center, 890 Oak Avenue

PROCESS:
1. Students transported to reunification site by staff (walking or bus as appropriate)
2. Teachers maintain class groups and take continuous attendance
3. ParentSquare notification sent with reunification location and instructions
4. Parents/guardians enter through CHECK-IN area with photo ID
5. Staff verify identity against emergency contact list in PowerSchool
6. Student released only to authorized adults on emergency contact list
7. Parent signs release log with time
8. Students without pickup remain with staff until all students are claimed
9. Unaccompanied students: Contact emergency contacts in order listed; if unreachable after 2 hours, contact Ramsey County child protection

13. BUILDING SECURITY
====================================================================

- All exterior doors locked during school hours (8:15 AM - 3:45 PM)
- Main entrance: Buzzer entry with visual verification via camera
- All visitors must present photo ID and be logged in Raptor visitor management system
- Background check run through Raptor for all visitors against sex offender registries
- Security cameras: 16 exterior cameras, 8 interior cameras (hallways, entrances, parking lot)
- Camera footage retained for 30 days
- Playground fully fenced with self-closing gates
- After-hours access: Key fob entry for authorized staff only; access log maintained

14. VISITOR MANAGEMENT
====================================================================

All visitors must:
1. Enter through main entrance only
2. Present government-issued photo ID
3. State purpose of visit
4. Be scanned through Raptor visitor management system
5. Receive and display visitor badge
6. Be escorted to destination by office staff or host teacher
7. Return badge and sign out upon departure

Exceptions: Law enforcement in uniform responding to a call

Volunteers: Must complete background check through school before first visit. Background checks valid for 2 years. Current volunteers with cleared backgrounds: 28.
