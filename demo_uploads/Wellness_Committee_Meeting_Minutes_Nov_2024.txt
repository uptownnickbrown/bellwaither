2. PHYSICAL ACTIVITY UPDATE
====================================================================

PE Teacher reported on physical activity programming:

Current provision:
- K-5: 90 minutes/week of PE (2 sessions of 45 min)
- 6-8: 90 minutes/week of PE (2 sessions of 45 min)
- Daily recess: K-2 (30 min), 3-4 (20 min), 5 (15 min), 6-8 (none - lunch only)

Discussion points:
- Minnesota recommends 150 minutes/week of physical activity for elementary students and LIA currently provides 90 minutes of PE. The gap should be partially filled by recess but K-2 is the only grade band meeting the combined recommendation.
- Middle school students have no structured recess or physical activity break outside of PE days. The student focus group (not yet conducted at this point, but teachers have reported similar feedback) will likely raise this issue.
- PE Teacher advocated for classroom movement breaks to supplement PE time. Suggested Brain Breaks or GoNoodle resources.

Parent representative raised concern about outdoor recess in winter months. When wind chill drops below 0°F, indoor recess is held in classrooms, which provides no meaningful physical activity. Suggested partnering with YMCA for indoor active play options during extreme cold.

DeShawn Carter (YMCA) offered to provide indoor activity resources for classroom teachers and noted that after-school program includes daily active play time (5:00-5:30 PM).

ACTION: PE Teacher to create "Classroom Movement Breaks" resource guide for all teachers by December 1. Derek Olson to establish clear indoor recess protocol with active options. Dr. Rivera to explore feasibility of a middle school "activity break" in the schedule for 2025-26.

3. NUTRITION AND SCHOOL MEALS
====================================================================

Derek Olson reported on school nutrition:

Meal participation trends:
- Breakfast participation has increased from 65% to 72% since implementing universal free breakfast in September (funded through SBP). Anecdotal teacher reports suggest fewer students arriving hungry.
- Lunch participation stable at 88%.
- Fresh Fruit and Vegetable Program (K-5) well-received. Students particularly enjoy the exposure to new fruits they haven't tried before.

Challenges:
- Food waste remains high, particularly with vegetable sides. Cafeteria staff estimate 30% of vegetables served are discarded.
- Several families have requested culturally relevant meal options. Current menu has limited variety.
- Kitchen equipment is aging (dishwasher nearing end of life, as noted in facility assessment).

Parent representative asked about the possibility of adopting Community Eligibility Provision (CEP) to provide free meals to ALL students, eliminating the application process and stigma. Derek confirmed the school qualifies and this will be discussed at a board meeting for potential 2025-26 implementation.

Tamara Williams noted that several Somali and Halal-observant families have expressed concern about meal options. Currently, families can request a vegetarian alternative, but a more intentionally inclusive menu would be appreciated.

ACTION: Derek to research CEP implementation process and present to board by February. Cafeteria to pilot one new culturally relevant meal option per month starting January. Explore partnerships with local food programs for culturally diverse menu consultation.

4. MENTAL HEALTH AND SEL
====================================================================

Rachel Washington and Tuan Pham provided an update on social-emotional learning and mental health supports:

Current programming:
- Panorama SEL survey administered fall 2024 (results shared school-wide at November staff meeting)
- SEL curriculum (Second Step) implemented in K-5 with weekly 30-minute lessons
- Advisory period in 6-8 includes SEL components (community circles, identity work)
- Individual counseling caseload: 28 students receiving regular counseling services (Rachel: 14, Tuan: 14)
- MSW intern (Aisha Mohammed) providing additional counseling capacity (8 students, 3 days/week)

Concerns:
- Student anxiety and depression symptoms have increased, particularly in grades 6-8. Counselors are at capacity with current caseloads.
- 4 students referred to outside mental health providers this fall. Wait times for community providers are 6-8 weeks.
- MSW intern ends placement in May with no guaranteed replacement for fall.

Discussion about the need for on-site mental health services, potentially through a community partnership. Tuan Pham noted that several charter schools in the Twin Cities have partnered with Washburn Center for Children or Headway Emotional Health Services to provide on-site therapy. This would not replace school counselors but would add clinical mental health capacity.

The committee discussed the impact of social media on middle school student mental health. Several teachers have reported increased social drama related to online interactions. The current phone policy (phones collected at start of day) helps during school hours but doesn't address the broader issue.

Parent representative shared that other schools offer parent education sessions on social media and mental health. This could be a family engagement opportunity.

ACTION: Tuan Pham to research on-site mental health partnership options and present costs/models by January. Rachel Washington to develop a parent education session on youth mental health and social media for February. Dr. Rivera to advocate for MSW intern position in next year's budget rather than relying solely on the partnership with U of St. Thomas.

5. STAFF WELLNESS
====================================================================

Dr. Rivera raised the importance of staff wellness, noting that staff satisfaction survey data showed work-life balance as the lowest-rated category (average 3.0/5.0).

Current staff wellness offerings:
- YMCA discounted membership (12 staff participating)
- Quarterly staff social events (potlucks, team-building)
- Employee Assistance Program (EAP) through benefits provider
- Two personal days per year in addition to sick leave

Discussion:
- Several committee members noted that teacher workload has increased with new initiatives and that "wellness" should start with manageable expectations, not just add-on programs.
- PE Teacher suggested offering a weekly staff yoga or movement session before or after school. Volunteered to lead a pilot session.
- Parent representative asked if staff wellness is included in budget planning. Dr. Rivera noted it is not currently a line item but should be considered.

ACTION: PE Teacher to pilot weekly 30-minute staff wellness session (yoga/movement) starting January. Dr. Rivera to review initiative load on teachers with an eye toward reducing or consolidating. EAP information to be re-shared with all staff (many don't know about it). Consider adding staff wellness budget line for 2025-26.
