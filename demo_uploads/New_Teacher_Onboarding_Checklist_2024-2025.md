## Pre-Employment (Upon Signed Offer Letter)

### Administrative/HR
- [ ] Background check completed and cleared (required by MN statute)
- [ ] Signed employment contract returned
- [ ] W-4 and direct deposit forms submitted
- [ ] Benefits enrollment completed (health, dental, vision, retirement - 30-day enrollment window)
- [ ] Emergency contact information submitted
- [ ] Copy of teaching license on file (verify active Minnesota license)
- [ ] Transcripts on file
- [ ] Mandatory reporter acknowledgment signed
- [ ] Handbook acknowledgment signed
- [ ] Technology acceptable use policy signed
- [ ] Photo submitted for staff directory and ID badge
- [ ] Parking pass issued (if applicable)

### Building Access
- [ ] Key fob programmed and issued
- [ ] Classroom key issued
- [ ] Mailbox assigned
- [ ] Classroom assignment confirmed

### Technology Setup
- [ ] Staff laptop issued (Dell Latitude)
- [ ] Google Workspace account created (@lincolninnovation.org)
- [ ] PowerSchool SIS account created with appropriate permissions
- [ ] ParentSquare account created
- [ ] GoGuardian teacher dashboard access
- [ ] Panorama Education teacher login
- [ ] Seesaw (K-2) or Google Classroom (3-8) account configured
- [ ] Copier code assigned
- [ ] Wi-Fi credentials provided
- [ ] Interactive display training scheduled (Kevin Park, Tech Specialist)

## Summer Onboarding (August, Pre-Service Week)

### Day 1: Welcome and Orientation (Full Day)
- [ ] Welcome breakfast with leadership team
- [ ] Building tour including emergency exits, nurse's office, supply room, staff lounge, copy room
- [ ] Meet office staff, custodial team, counselors
- [ ] Review school history, mission, and vision
- [ ] Strategic plan overview and current priorities
- [ ] Review School Quality Framework (SQF) and how LIA uses it
- [ ] Organizational chart and communication norms review
- [ ] Lunch with grade-level/department team

### Day 2: Policies, Procedures, and Culture (Full Day)
- [ ] Staff handbook review (attendance reporting, dress code, communication expectations)
- [ ] Emergency procedures training (fire, lockdown, severe weather, medical)
- [ ] Locate and review red emergency folder for your classroom
- [ ] Behavior management philosophy and restorative practices overview
- [ ] Student culture expectations (PRIDE values: Perseverance, Respect, Innovation, Discipline, Empathy)
- [ ] Mandatory reporter training (online module + in-person discussion)
- [ ] Blood-borne pathogen training
- [ ] Review bell schedule, duty assignments, and supervision expectations
- [ ] Tour of neighborhood (community context walk)

### Day 3: Curriculum and Instruction (Full Day)
- [ ] Curriculum overview for your grade/subject area
- [ ] Scope and sequence review with instructional coach
- [ ] Assessment calendar and data systems walkthrough (MAP, FAST, MCA)
- [ ] Lesson planning expectations and template review
- [ ] Observation and evaluation process overview (Danielson Framework)
- [ ] MTSS/RTI framework and intervention block overview
- [ ] Special education overview: IEP at-a-glance documents for your students, co-teaching expectations
- [ ] ELL supports overview: WIDA levels, sheltered instruction strategies
- [ ] Technology integration expectations (DreamBox, Lexia, Newsela, etc.)

### Day 4: Community and Relationships (Half Day)
- [ ] Family engagement expectations and communication norms
- [ ] ParentSquare training (weekly newsletter template, messaging families)
- [ ] Back-to-School Night preparation guidance
- [ ] Review family survey data and community demographics
- [ ] Meet mentor teacher and establish initial meeting schedule
- [ ] Goal-setting conversation with principal (30 minutes, one-on-one)
- [ ] Classroom setup time (afternoon)

### Day 5: Team Planning and Preparation (Full Day)
- [ ] Grade-level/department team planning session (norms, meeting schedule, shared responsibilities)
- [ ] Co-plan first week of instruction with team/mentor
- [ ] Set up classroom library, bulletin boards, student materials
- [ ] Prepare welcome letter for families
- [ ] Review class rosters, IEP/504 accommodations, and relevant student information
- [ ] Finalize first two weeks of lesson plans
- [ ] Practice classroom technology (interactive display, document camera)

## First Two Weeks of School

### Week 1 (Student Start)
- [ ] Implement classroom community-building activities (community circle, name activities, class norms creation)
- [ ] Establish routines and procedures explicitly (practice transitions, materials management, signals)
- [ ] Administer beginning-of-year assessments as scheduled
- [ ] Send welcome communication to all families via ParentSquare
- [ ] Daily debrief with mentor teacher (15-20 minutes)
- [ ] Attend Welcome Back to School Night (typically Thursday of Week 1)
- [ ] Complete student information review for all students with IEPs, 504s, or behavior plans

### Week 2
- [ ] Begin regular instruction per scope and sequence
- [ ] Complete MAP Fall testing (if assigned to your class)
- [ ] Hold 1:1 check-in with principal (15 minutes)
- [ ] Attend first grade-level team meeting
- [ ] Begin weekly communication to families (newsletter or update via ParentSquare)
- [ ] Submit Week 3-4 lesson plans to instructional coach for feedback

## First Month (September)

- [ ] Receive first formal classroom observation (unannounced) with post-conference
- [ ] Complete all required online training modules (SafeSchools platform):
  - Sexual harassment prevention
  - Cybersecurity awareness
  - Suicide prevention awareness
  - Restraint and seclusion (if applicable to role)
- [ ] Attend September staff meeting and professional development sessions
- [ ] Establish intervention groups based on fall assessment data
- [ ] Attend first data team meeting with instructional coach
- [ ] Complete first progress monitoring check for intervention students
- [ ] Join at least one school committee (Equity Committee, Wellness Committee, PBL Committee, or Culture Committee)
- [ ] Monthly check-in with mentor teacher: review goals, discuss challenges, celebrate wins

---

## First Quarter (August - October)

- [ ] Complete 2 coaching cycles with instructional coach (observation, debrief, goal-setting)
- [ ] Attend parent-teacher conferences (October) - prep meeting with mentor beforehand
- [ ] Submit Q1 grades and progress reports on time
- [ ] Participate in first PBL unit (observe/co-facilitate if new to PBL)
- [ ] Complete CPR/First Aid certification (if not current)
- [ ] Monthly check-ins with principal (October: formal mid-quarter review)
- [ ] Read and reflect on one professional text with mentor (suggested: "Culturally Responsive Teaching and the Brain" by Hammond)

---

## First Semester (August - January)

- [ ] Complete 4 coaching cycles total with instructional coach
- [ ] Receive mid-year formal evaluation (Danielson rubric)
- [ ] Participate in mid-year data review (January PD day)
- [ ] Adjust intervention groups based on winter assessment data
- [ ] Attend all required PD sessions and complete reflection logs
- [ ] Observe 2 veteran teachers (arranged by instructional coach)
- [ ] Complete mid-year self-reflection using school's reflection template
- [ ] Mid-year check-in with principal: review evaluation, adjust goals

## Mentor Teacher Program

Each new teacher (0-2 years experience at LIA) is assigned a mentor teacher. Mentors are veteran LIA teachers with strong evaluation records who volunteer for this role.

### Mentor Responsibilities
- Meet weekly with mentee (minimum 30 minutes) during first semester
- Meet bi-weekly during second semester
- Observe mentee teaching at least 2 times per semester (informal, supportive)
- Be observed by mentee at least 2 times per semester
- Help mentee navigate school culture, logistics, and relationships
- Provide emotional support and encouragement
- Maintain confidentiality (mentor conversations are not evaluative)
